Sports Analytics Organizations

Communities & Conferences to Get Involved In

Getting involved in the sports analytics community accelerates career growth, keeps you current with the field, and builds the network that opens doors. These are the organizations and events worth knowing.

Professional Organizations

  • Society for American Baseball Research (SABR) — the original sports analytics community; home of the Analytics Conference each spring and the Analytics chapter. Membership is open to all levels.
  • Sports Analytics Association (SAA) — practitioner-focused community hosting webinars, job resources, and the annual Sports Analytics World Series conference
  • Harvard Sports Analysis Collective (HSAC) — student-run organization producing original research; a model for building an analytics culture in academic settings
  • Analytics at Wharton — hosts the Wharton Sports Analytics and Business Initiative (WSABI) and publishes practitioner-focused research
  • Sigma (formerly HBSA) — Hockey analytics community with an annual conference and a welcoming entry point for hockey-specific work

Flagship Conferences

  • MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference (SSAC) — the highest-profile annual conference in sports analytics; held each spring in Boston. Includes a research paper competition.
  • SABR Analytics Conference — baseball-focused; held annually in Phoenix. Strong mix of academics, front-office staff, and media
  • Carnegie Mellon Sports Analytics Conference (CMSAC) — methods-focused academic conference; good venue for presenting original statistical work
  • Ontario Universities Sports Analytics Conference (OUSAC) — OUSAC unites the brightest student minds with industry leaders from the MLB, NHL, NBA, and top analytics firms. Experience invited talks, panels, and our signature data challenge showcase
  • Ohio State Sports Analytics Conference) — a free, student-oriented three-day event combining virtual and in-person formats to bring together student researchers, hackathon competitors, and industry guests from organizations like ESPN, the Cleveland Cavaliers, FOX Sports, and Sports Info Solutions for presentations and panels on sports analytics

Online Communities

  • Reddit: r/sportsanalytics — general discussion, project sharing, and career questions
  • Open Source Sports — GitHub organization aggregating open sports data projects
  • Slack & Discord communities — many sport-specific analytics communities (e.g., nflverse, StatsBomb) maintain active Slack or Discord servers for package users and contributors

Job Boards & Career Resources

  • TeamWork Online — the primary job board for sports industry positions, including analytics and data science roles
  • Sports Business Journal Jobs — broader sports industry postings with analytics roles
  • LinkedIn — follow team analytics departments and front-office staff directly; many positions are posted here before anywhere else
